((F - 32) * 5) / 9

Rate of climb in fpm for takeoff minimums

TAKEOFF MINIMUMS: Rwy 3, 300-1 or std. w/ min. climb
of 451' per NM to 1300. Rwy 28, 500-3 or std. w/ min.
climb of 295' per NM to 1600.

fpm = (ground speed / 60) * (feet per NM) or (ratio 300/1)

Calculated for runway 3

fpm = (70 / 60) * 451 = 526.16
fpm = 1.16 * 451 = 526.16

Calculated for runway 28

fpm = (70 / 60) * 295 = 342.20
fpm = 1.16 * 295 = 342.20

Rate of descent
rate of descent in fpm = tan(glide slope) * (speed in knots * 6076 / 60)

4.0 degree glide
fpm = tan(4.0) * ((70 * 6076) / 60)
fpm = 0.069926 * (425,320 / 60)
fpm = 0.069926 * 7,088.666667
fpm = 495.68
=======================================================================
Rate of descent for 3.0 degree glide slope (Rule of thumb)
fpm = (airspeed / 2) * 10
Flying a landing at 70 knots
fpm = (70 / 2) * 10
fpm = 35 * 10 = 350

Cloudbase For Celsius

For Fahrenheit
cloud base = ((temperature - dewpoint) / 4.4) * 1000

First, we need pressure altitude which can be easily calculated.
OAT is “Outside Air Temperature” in degrees Celsius and ISA Temp is always 15 °C at sea level.
To find ISA standard temperature for a given altitude, double the altitude, subtract 15 and place a – sign in front of it.
